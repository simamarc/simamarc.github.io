The digital postcard app

Years ago, a holiday wasn’t complete until you sent postcards to friends and family to let them know you were having a better time than they were. I created a mobile phone app called “The Digital Postcard”. The app will allow users to create their own postcard by using a photograph from their phone library, the card will be printed in the home country of the recipient and then posted to the recipient’s address. The app I did for David Travis course "User experience: The Ultimate Guide to Usability" on Udemy.

The problem. Travel postcards are usually quite old school and not personal. Also, it is time-consuming to find the right one, a stamp and a postbox.

I tried to reduce complexity in the app, so that the consumer is not confused with what action you want them to take. Also, I added an option to randomise your photo to make it more personal. After prototyping, testing and getting the feedback, I found that people want to have the ability to add more addresses and to pay instantly with the phone without filling the blanks.

Gridualism branding and UX/UI design

Explore European statistical datasets through interactive charts. I created branding and landing page.

Gradualism, from the Latin gradus ("step"), is the principle or policy of achieving some goal by gradual steps rather than by drastic change. I chose to represent this through the symmetrical, repetitive shape of the icon of a logo. Also, the name of a brand is made of grid + dual. So, I used a grid pattern made of two lines to complement it. Since statistics could be hard to understand, I tried to reduce complexity in overall branding and web design.

The problem. It is hard to keep the attention of the customer with the complex data. Also, we want to make statistics look hot. In other words, to attract interest, new customers and be visually appealing.

Todo app

The app I did for Udemy course "Design rules: fundamental visual principles + practices for great UI design".

The problem. Too many features in to do apps make managing to do lists complicated.

After carrying out the user research and making surveys I clarified my goals and tools for "Todo" app: find the key user group, identify the key tasks. The key group is people who care about productivity and making the most of their time. It means that the app should be really easy to use. I prioritised the key task without adding irrelevant features. After prototyping, testing and getting the feedback, I found that people want to have the ability to scroll through the different days easily.

BDP branding

BDP ("Baltic digital printing") is one of the biggest large format printing companies in Lithuania that provides print and promotional solutions to a wide range of industries. The main qualities of the company: ambition (willing to take complicated tasks), speed (open 24 hours a day), trustworthiness (10 years of experience, trusted by big brands not cutting on quality).

The big part of this job is still in the hands of people: discussing, designing, preparing for print, cutting, delivering, installing. So, I used a symbol of origami - for the love of paper, handwork and human's dedication in the form of craft. The seagull represents adaptability, resourcefulness, and opportunities. Its meaning focuses on the need to find ways to survive and do even the uncomfortable to succeed. I chose calm blue, gray tones with a hint of orange (one of the qualities is ambition after all) and complimented it with strict lines symbolising professionalism in craft, attention to detail.
